Young-Baxter Operators

Summary of Young-Baxter Operators (YBOs)

Young-Baxter Operators (YBOs) are crucial in areas like statistical mechanics, quantum groups, and knot theory. They satisfy the Yang-Baxter equation:

$$R_{12} R_{13} R_{23} = R_{23} R_{13} R_{12}$$

This equation governs the interaction between three particles or states in a system.

Algebraic Ansatz

Classifying Yang-Baxter operators is a crucial step in the study of simulating integrable quantum systems on quantum computers. Hietarinta's early work focused on classifying constant Yang-Baxter solutions for a two-dimensional Hilbert space (qubit representation), identifying 11 families of invertible solutions, including one derived from the permutation operator. While effective for \(4 \times 4\) systems, this method becomes impractical for higher-dimensional representations. We address this limitation by constructing constant Yang-Baxter solutions in a representation-independent manner, utilizing ansätze from algebraic structures.
